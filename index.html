  About

What is MIDHEM?

A half-day workshop at the intersection of remote sensing, deep learning, and environmental science.

MIDHEM brings together researchers and practitioners working at the intersection of image processing, remote sensing, and environmental sciences. The workshop addresses the growing need for advanced imaging and AI techniques to monitor, model, and predict hydrological and environmental phenomena — from flood mapping to glacier dynamics and water-quality assessment.

Building on the rich heritage of Earth Observation satellites (Sentinel, Landsat, MODIS) and the rapid advances in deep learning, large language models (LLMs), and multi-modal fusion, MIDHEM fosters cross-disciplinary dialogue between the image processing community and environmental scientists.

The workshop welcomes contributions on novel methods, benchmark datasets, open-data initiatives, and real-world applications that demonstrate the power of imaging data in hydrological and environmental modelling.

  Call for Papers

Submit Your Research

We invite contributions on methods, applications, and open-data initiatives at the frontier of imaging and environmental science.

  Topics of Interest

Methods & Models

  • Deep learning for remote sensing image analysis
  • Multi-modal and multi-temporal data fusion (SAR + Optical)
  • Foundation models and LLMs for geospatial data
  • Super-resolution and image reconstruction techniques
  • Generative models for data augmentation and simulation
  • Self-supervised and unsupervised learning methods
  • Uncertainty quantification in environmental predictions

Applications

  • Flood detection, mapping, and risk assessment
  • Glacier and sea-ice monitoring from satellite data
  • Water body extraction and water quality estimation
  • Vegetation and land-cover change detection
  • Urban heat island and climate adaptation
  • Precipitation and soil moisture estimation
  • Digital twin environments for water cycle modelling

Open Data & Benchmarks

  • Novel benchmark datasets for hydrological imaging
  • Open-source tools and reproducible pipelines
  • Large-scale annotation efforts and crowdsourcing

Submission Guidelines

Paper Length 4 pages maximum (excluding references), following IEEE ICIP 2026 paper template
Format IEEE two-column format; LaTeX and Word templates available via IEEE Author Center
Double-Blind Review Submissions must be anonymized; do not include author names or affiliations
Proceedings Accepted papers will be published in IEEExplore as part of ICIP 2026 proceedings
Presentation At least one author must register and present at the workshop in person
